## Overview

Consumer theory provides a rigorous framework for understanding how individuals make choices. We model consumers as rational agents who maximize utility subject to a budget constraint.

Key questions:

- What does it mean for a consumer to be "rational"?
- How do we represent preferences mathematically?
- How does a consumer choose the best affordable bundle?
- How do changes in prices and income affect consumption?

## Topics

- Preference relations and their properties (completeness, transitivity, monotonicity, convexity)
- Utility functions as representations of preferences
- Budget sets and budget constraints
- Utility maximization
- The Marshallian (ordinary) demand function
- Comparative statics: income and substitution effects
- The Slutsky equation
- Compensated (Hicksian) demand and expenditure minimization
- Duality between utility maximization and expenditure minimization
