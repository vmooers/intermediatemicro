11.1 Overview

A monopoly is a market with a single seller who has market power — the ability to set prices above marginal cost. This chapter analyzes monopoly behavior and its welfare consequences.

11.2 Topics

11.2.1 Monopoly Behavior

  • Sources of market power: barriers to entry, patents, natural monopoly
  • The monopolist’s demand curve (downward sloping)
  • Revenue: \(R(q) = p(q) \cdot q\)
  • Marginal revenue: \(MR = p + q \cdot \frac{dp}{dq} < p\)
  • Profit maximization: \(MR = MC\)
  • Lerner Index of market power: \(\frac{p - MC}{p} = -\frac{1}{\varepsilon}\)

11.2.2 Monopoly Welfare

  • Deadweight loss from monopoly
  • Comparison with competitive outcome
  • Efficiency and equity concerns

11.2.3 Price Discrimination

  • First-degree (perfect) price discrimination
  • Second-degree price discrimination (nonlinear pricing, two-part tariffs)
  • Third-degree price discrimination (market segmentation)
    • Condition: \(MR_1 = MR_2 = MC\)
    • Higher price in the market with less elastic demand

11.2.4 Natural Monopoly and Regulation

  • Natural monopoly: subadditive costs
  • Regulation: marginal cost pricing vs. average cost pricing
  • Rate-of-return regulation
