Intermediate Microeconomics

Chapter 13 Resources

This chapter collects links to useful external resources for Intermediate Microeconomics. These include free textbooks, lecture notes, video lectures, mathematical tools, and data sources.

13.1 Textbooks

13.1.1 Primary

  • Varian, Hal R. — Intermediate Microeconomics: A Modern Approach (W.W. Norton) The standard undergraduate intermediate micro textbook. A companion Workouts book provides additional problems.

13.1.2 Free / Open-Access Textbooks

  • OpenStax Microeconomics — A free, peer-reviewed introductory/intermediate textbook.
  • Principles of Economics (Libretexts) — Open-access economics textbooks and notes.
  • Varian — Microeconomic Analysis — The graduate-level companion to Varian’s undergraduate text (advanced reference).

13.2 Lecture Notes

  • MIT OpenCourseWare — 14.01 Principles of Microeconomics — Lecture notes and problem sets from MIT’s introductory micro course.
  • MIT OpenCourseWare — 14.03 Microeconomic Theory and Public Policy — Intermediate-level notes.
  • Penn State — ECON 301 Lecture Notes — Freely available lecture notes for intermediate micro.

13.3 Video Lectures

  • Khan Academy — Microeconomics — Short video lessons covering core micro concepts.
  • Marginal Revolution University — Free video-based micro course by Tyler Cowen and Alex Tabarrok.
  • Yale — ECON 159: Game Theory (Ben Polak) — Full lecture series on game theory from Yale’s Open Yale Courses.

13.4 Mathematical Tools

  • Paul’s Online Math Notes — Calculus — Excellent free reference for calculus, including partial derivatives and optimization.
  • Math for Economists (Simon & Blume) — A standard math methods textbook for economics students.
  • Khan Academy — Multivariable Calculus — Free videos on partial derivatives, gradients, and Lagrange multipliers.

13.5 Problem Sets and Practice

  • Varian Workouts in Intermediate Microeconomics — The standard problem book accompanying Varian’s text.
  • Economics Network — Problem Sets — Resources for economics students and educators in the UK.

13.6 Data and Empirical Applications

  • FRED Economic Data (St. Louis Fed) — Macroeconomic and microeconomic data series.
  • Bureau of Labor Statistics — Data on prices, employment, wages, and more.
  • Census Bureau — American Community Survey — Household and individual-level data for empirical applications.

13.7 Online Encyclopedias and References

  • Investopedia — Economics — Plain-language explanations of economic concepts.
  • The Library of Economics and Liberty (Econlib) — Encyclopedia of Economics and Liberty, including The Concise Encyclopedia of Economics.

13.8 R and Reproducible Research

Since this book is built with R Markdown and bookdown, students interested in the tools used are encouraged to explore:

  • bookdown: Authoring Books and Technical Documents with R Markdown — The official bookdown documentation.
  • R for Data Science — Free online book for learning R and data analysis.
  • RStudio — The standard IDE for R.
