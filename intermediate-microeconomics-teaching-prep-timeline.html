1.7 DETAILED CANVAS/ACHIEVE INTEGRATION GUIDE

1.7.1 Understanding the Integration

What is LTI? - Learning Tools Interoperability - standard protocol for external tools to integrate with Canvas - Achieve uses LTI to connect with Canvas - Once set up, students never need to log into Achieve separately

How it Works: 1. OU bookstore sets up student Achieve access (through Inclusive Access) 2. You install Achieve LTI app in your Canvas course 3. You link Achieve assignments to Canvas modules 4. Students click assignment in Canvas → automatically logged into Achieve 5. Students complete homework in Achieve 6. Grades automatically sync to Canvas gradebook 7. You can view student progress in both Canvas and Achieve instructor dashboard

Benefits: - ✅ Students access everything through one place (Canvas) - ✅ Automatic grading - saves you hours per week - ✅ Grades automatically in Canvas gradebook - ✅ Students can’t claim they “couldn’t find” homework - ✅ Built-in due date reminders through Canvas

1.7.2 Common Issues & Solutions

Issue: Students can’t access Achieve - Check: Did they opt out of Inclusive Access? (Need to purchase separately) - Check: Did they click through Canvas, not go to Achieve directly? - Solution: Have them contact OU bookstore to reinstate Inclusive Access

Issue: Grades not showing in Canvas - Check: Did you enable grade passback when creating assignment? - Check: Is Achieve assignment worth points in Canvas? - Solution: Edit assignment settings, ensure “Sync Grades” is enabled

Issue: Student can’t see assignment - Check: Is module published? - Check: Is assignment published? - Check: Are there date restrictions on the assignment?

Issue: Assignment link doesn’t work - Check: Did Achieve app lose connection? May need to reinstall LTI - Solution: Go to Settings → Apps → Find Achieve → Reinstall

1.9.3 On Interactive Teaching Format

Your colleague’s advice: “Students responded best to a course format where I work through lots of examples in class and give students time to work on their own rather than a lecture-only format.”

Implementation tips: - Plan 2-3 worked examples per class period - After each example, give a similar problem for students to try (5-10 min) - Walk around, answer questions while they work - Discuss solutions together - This makes 50-minute classes fly by! - Time management: Don’t over-prepare content - interactive time fills quickly

Typical class structure: 1. Brief concept review (10 min) 2. Work through Example 1 (10 min) 3. Students practice similar problem (7 min) 4. Discuss solution (5 min) 5. Work through Example 2 (10 min) 6. Students practice (7 min) 7. Wrap-up/questions (3 min)

1.9.4 On Hal Varian Textbook

How to use it: - For you: Deeper mathematical understanding, alternative explanations - For students: Recommend as optional supplement for math-oriented students - In class: “If you want to see a more mathematical treatment, check Varian Chapter X” - On exams: Can include more mathematical problems (but also more applied ones)

Key differences: - Varian: More abstract, proof-based, mathematical rigor - Goolsbee: More applied, empirical examples, real-world focus - Your approach: Use Goolsbee as main text, reference Varian for deeper theory

1.9.5 On Glen Weyl Materials

You do NOT need permission to: - Use his public YouTube videos as supplementary resources - Recommend them to students as optional viewing - Watch them yourself for pedagogical ideas - Use pedagogical approaches you observe

Best approach: - List Weyl videos as “optional supplementary lectures” on syllabus/bookdown - Link to them in relevant chapters - Students appreciate having alternative explanations - Send courtesy email thanking him (optional but nice)

1.9.6 On Bookdown Development

Keep it simple your first time: - Think of it as your personal course notebook - Write chapter notes as you prep - Add materials gradually - Link to resources as you find them - Don’t stress about making it perfect
