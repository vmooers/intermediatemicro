## Overview

Oligopoly markets are characterized by a small number of strategically interacting firms. Unlike perfect competition or monopoly, the outcomes in oligopoly depend on the specific model of strategic behavior firms are assumed to adopt.

## Topics

### Models of Oligopoly

#### Cournot Competition (Quantity Competition)
- Firms simultaneously choose quantities
- Best response functions
- Cournot-Nash equilibrium
- Comparison with monopoly and competitive outcomes
- With $n$ firms: as $n \to \infty$, Cournot approaches competitive outcome

#### Bertrand Competition (Price Competition)
- Firms simultaneously choose prices
- Bertrand paradox: with homogeneous goods and two firms, $p = MC$
- Resolution: capacity constraints, differentiated products, repeated interaction

#### Stackelberg Leadership
- Sequential quantity competition
- Leader moves first; follower best-responds
- Solved by backward induction
- Leader produces more than in Cournot; follower produces less

### Collusion

- Incentives for firms to collude (joint profit maximization)
- Instability of collusion: incentive to deviate
- Collusion as a repeated game equilibrium

### Product Differentiation

- Differentiated Bertrand competition
- Hotelling model of spatial competition
