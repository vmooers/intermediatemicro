# Resources {#resources}

This chapter collects links to useful external resources for Intermediate Microeconomics. These include free textbooks, lecture notes, video lectures, mathematical tools, and data sources.

## Textbooks

### Primary
- **Varian, Hal R. — *Intermediate Microeconomics: A Modern Approach*** (W.W. Norton)
  The standard undergraduate intermediate micro textbook. A companion *Workouts* book provides additional problems.

### Free / Open-Access Textbooks
- [**OpenStax Microeconomics**](https://openstax.org/details/books/microeconomics-3e) — A free, peer-reviewed introductory/intermediate textbook.
- [**Principles of Economics (Libretexts)**](https://socialsci.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Economics) — Open-access economics textbooks and notes.
- [**Varian — *Microeconomic Analysis***](https://wwnorton.com/books/9780393957358) — The graduate-level companion to Varian's undergraduate text (advanced reference).

## Lecture Notes

- [**MIT OpenCourseWare — 14.01 Principles of Microeconomics**](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/14-01-principles-of-microeconomics-fall-2018/) — Lecture notes and problem sets from MIT's introductory micro course.
- [**MIT OpenCourseWare — 14.03 Microeconomic Theory and Public Policy**](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/14-03-microeconomic-theory-and-public-policy-fall-2016/) — Intermediate-level notes.
- [**Penn State — ECON 301 Lecture Notes**](https://www.personal.psu.edu/dxl31/econ301/) — Freely available lecture notes for intermediate micro.

## Video Lectures

- [**Khan Academy — Microeconomics**](https://www.khanacademy.org/economics-finance-domain/microeconomics) — Short video lessons covering core micro concepts.
- [**Marginal Revolution University**](https://mru.org/courses/principles-economics-microeconomics) — Free video-based micro course by Tyler Cowen and Alex Tabarrok.
- [**Yale — ECON 159: Game Theory (Ben Polak)**](https://oyc.yale.edu/economics/econ-159) — Full lecture series on game theory from Yale's Open Yale Courses.

## Mathematical Tools

- [**Paul's Online Math Notes — Calculus**](https://tutorial.math.lamar.edu/) — Excellent free reference for calculus, including partial derivatives and optimization.
- [**Math for Economists (Simon & Blume)**](https://wwnorton.com/books/9780393117523) — A standard math methods textbook for economics students.
- [**Khan Academy — Multivariable Calculus**](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/multivariable-calculus) — Free videos on partial derivatives, gradients, and Lagrange multipliers.

## Problem Sets and Practice

- [**Varian Workouts in Intermediate Microeconomics**](https://wwnorton.com/books/9780393922615) — The standard problem book accompanying Varian's text.
- [**Economics Network — Problem Sets**](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/) — Resources for economics students and educators in the UK.

## Data and Empirical Applications

- [**FRED Economic Data (St. Louis Fed)**](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/) — Macroeconomic and microeconomic data series.
- [**Bureau of Labor Statistics**](https://www.bls.gov/) — Data on prices, employment, wages, and more.
- [**Census Bureau — American Community Survey**](https://www.census.gov/programs-surveys/acs) — Household and individual-level data for empirical applications.

## Online Encyclopedias and References

- [**Investopedia — Economics**](https://www.investopedia.com/economics-4689800) — Plain-language explanations of economic concepts.
- [**The Library of Economics and Liberty (Econlib)**](https://www.econlib.org/) — Encyclopedia of Economics and Liberty, including *The Concise Encyclopedia of Economics*.

## R and Reproducible Research

Since this book is built with R Markdown and bookdown, students interested in the tools used are encouraged to explore:

- [**bookdown: Authoring Books and Technical Documents with R Markdown**](https://bookdown.org/yihui/bookdown/) — The official bookdown documentation.
- [**R for Data Science**](https://r4ds.hadley.nz/) — Free online book for learning R and data analysis.
- [**RStudio**](https://posit.co/download/rstudio-desktop/) — The standard IDE for R.
