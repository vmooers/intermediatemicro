# Introduction {-}

Welcome to the course materials for **Intermediate Microeconomics**. This book serves as a central hub for lecture notes, problem sets, supplemental readings, and other resources related to the course.

## About This Course

Intermediate Microeconomics builds on the foundations of introductory economics to develop a more rigorous and mathematical framework for understanding how individuals, firms, and markets make decisions. Topics covered include:

- **Consumer Theory**: How individuals make choices given preferences and budget constraints
- **Producer Theory**: How firms choose inputs and outputs to maximize profit
- **Market Structure**: Competitive markets, monopoly, oligopoly, and game theory
- **General Equilibrium**: How markets interact and reach economy-wide equilibrium

## How to Use This Book

The chapters in this book follow the general progression of the course. Each chapter contains:

- **Lecture Notes**: Key concepts and derivations from class
- **Examples**: Worked examples to illustrate the theory
- **Practice Problems**: Problems to test your understanding (solutions may be provided separately)

Use the table of contents on the left to navigate between chapters. The [Resources](#resources) chapter at the end contains links to external materials that may be helpful throughout the course.

## Prerequisites

Students should be comfortable with:

- Introductory Microeconomics (supply, demand, basic market analysis)
- Calculus (derivatives, partial derivatives, constrained optimization)
- Basic algebra and graphing
